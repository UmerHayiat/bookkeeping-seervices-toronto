What Does a Bookkeeper Do—and Why Does It Matter?

At its core, a bookkeeper tracks and organizes financial transactions, ensuring every dollar earned and spent is accurately recorded. They manage essential tasks such as:

  • Recording financial transactions.
  • Reconciling bank and credit card statements.
  • Managing accounts payable and receivable.
  • Preparing financial reports.
  • Collaborating with accountants for tax filings.
